We sincerely thank the reviewer for recognizing $SENSE$ as a "well-engineered combination." We gladly acknowledge that we draw profound inspiration from dense retrieval (e.g., DReSD) and relaxed acceptance mechanisms (e.g., ARC, FLY). However, we wish to clarify that SENSE is not merely a plug-and-play assembly of these techniques. Instead, its fundamental conceptual novelty lies in establishing a **Retrieval-Verification Co-design Principle for Speculative Decoding**.

To address your request for a more explicit and honest comparison, we elaborate on why this co-design is necessary and how our mechanism differs from prior arts:
1. The Interface Mismatch in Existing RSD: In existing Retrieval-based Speculative Decoding (RSD), whether utilizing lexical or dense retrievers, the verification phase rigidly relies on strict exact-matching starting from the very first token. In our empirical analysis, we observed that [XX]% of retrieved candidates fail this strict initial filter. This hard constraint fundamentally bottlenecks dense retrievers—wasting their inherent advantage in semantic matching (an advantage well-proven in RAG).
   Furthermore, SENSE does not solely rely on a pre-constructed global datastore. It dynamically constructs a temporary datastore mapping last-hidden-states to corresponding suffixes on the fly. This dual-source nature natively aligns with our dense computation and relaxed verification framework, maximizing both general and local context utilization.
2. Explicit Comparison with ARC and FLY: FLY是顺序的、因果的，基于后续token的匹配度决断当前token是否接受，我们的soft-gated mask combination是基于卷积融合周围token的匹配度，是并行的——这段还要想想怎么写。
3. Empirical Proof of Co-design: Our ablation studies explicitly prove that this is a necessary co-design rather than an A+B combination. When applied in isolation:
   1. SEN (Dense Retrieval) alone yields marginal gains because semantic-but-not-exact drafts are completely rejected by strict verification.
   2. SE (Soft-Verification) alone provides little expansion room because drafts that already passed standard strict-matching filtering leave little semantic variance to be "relaxed."
   
The true potential is only unlocked when dense retrieval and relaxed tree-verification are co-designed and deployed together, breaking the theoretical ceiling of isolated improvements.

In summary, SENSE’s core contribution is using a highly engineered architecture to prove a new paradigm: the necessity and immense potential of co-designing semantic retrieval with relaxed tree-verification. We will explicitly highlight this co-design principle and the detailed comparison with ARC/FLY in the revised manuscript.
